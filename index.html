Project Summary

Why this project?

Online museum collections make textile heritage accessible, yet many users struggle to move beyond search-and-retrieve toward deep understanding. Users often face information overload, high barriers to entry, and cross-cultural gaps where meanings can be situated, contested, and plural.

What we ask

"How can AI-mediated systems support cross-cultural sensemaking in textile heritage as a 'negotiation of meaning,' rather than delivering a single authoritative answer?"

What we contribute

  • Interaction designs for multi-voice, provenance-first interpretation
  • Evidence from speculative design + probes about trust and negotiation
  • A configurable prototype to compare, question, and keep alternatives

Interview Invitation

I am inviting museum, heritage, HCI, and digital heritage researchers and practitioners for a short online interview (~30–45 minutes).

The Goal

To understand how AI-mediated interpretation can better support cross-cultural comparison, provenance awareness, and plural narratives.

What It Involves

Discuss real-world cross-cultural sensemaking, AI expectations (authority, uncertainty), and suggestions for speculative design workshops.

Methods

01. Speculative Design

Explores future possibilities and societal impacts of emerging technology. We use this to explore futures of AI interpretation where meaning is negotiated rather than delivered. Participants work with prompts, artefact cards, and scenario-based activities.

02. Technology Probes

Technology probes implement selected interaction ideas as lightweight prototypes placed in realistic browsing tasks. The goal is to gain understanding of users and how well the proposed solution addresses their needs.

Pilot Workshop

A pilot speculative design workshop has been completed, establishing a baseline process framework.
